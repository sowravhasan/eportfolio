Collaborative Learning Discussion 1 - An analysis

This collaborative discussion required us to assess Compromising a Medical Mannequin. Healthcare Information Systems and Technology (Sighealth). By Glisson, W., Andel, T., McDonald, T., Jacobs, M., Campbell, M. & Mayr, J. (2015) .

The healthcare industry by nature is reliant on technology and medical devices are as susceptible to threats and vulnerabilities as any other device that is dependent on the use of the internet.

A recurring threat based on this paper is the risk of a brute force attack which in this context may be more likely if the as the medical mannequin front end platform is browser based. Therefore if the user interface is poorly designed this creates a vulnerability and potential entry point for a cyber-attack (Luo & Sogyazi , 2019). To mitigate against this I would recommend implementing stronger authentication methods using the theory of least privileges to ensure that only appropriate medical professionals can access the most critical configurations. Furthermore, this article highlighted the risks around physical security which can be often overlooked leading to malicious tampering of devices. I would mitigate against this by enforcing strict log out policies for computers which have access to the privileged medical data to protect confidentiality.

The methods mentioned above are both stemming from the CIA Triad which priorities Confidentially, Integrity and Availability when addressing information security (ForcePoint, 2018) and it is important to follow these guidelines which highlight practises in the industry.

What are the pros and cons of using Kali Linux vs. Nessus?

A main strength of Nessus is that due to its age it has the world’s largest vulnerability and configuration checks which are continuously updated (Tenable, 2017) . This ensures the inputs are as speedy and as accurate as possible. Systems are measured against the latest industry systems and are measured a standard for speed and accuracy. In the same breath, this could also be viewed as a con as it will require constant updates to ensure the most current vulnerabilities are mitigated against, thus taking away from the low maintenance nature. Kali Linux is, however. more user friendly and the scan can be done easily and more quickly. Also, it is easy to set up the scan with the output formatted in a way that is readable to all.

To conclude, deciding which tool is best to use is not a black or white matter. We have to take into consideration user and business priorities, budget time scales and what the ultimate outcome is that we want to achieve.

The name is taken from sonar terminology of sending a pulse of sound and listening for the object's echo underwater, is a network administration utility. Ping utility was first written by Mike Muuss in December 1983 for Ballistic Research Laboratory, now known as the US Army Research Laboratory. Ping utility is used to test reachability of a host and it measures the round-trip time for messages sent from the originating host to a destination on a network.

GDPR

The case study discusses the inappropriate storage of an individual’s medical data by their employer. There are a few key principles of the GDPR that this case study highlights such as the Data Minimisation principle. According to the Information Commissioner's Office (2019), Article 5(1)(c) of the GDPR legislation requires personal data held to be relevant and limited based on the initial purpose of the data. In this case the DoJ failed to periodically review the data held, if this step was taken they would have been more likely to notice that the complainants personal information was inappropriately stored. This can also be linked with the storage limitation principle that highlights data must not be held for longer that needed. Finally, the ‘integrity and confidentiality’ principle of the GDPR which reinforces the importance of properly securing data is also relevant.

In this case the data breach was resolved by removing the records from the database in question. The DoJ also provided an apology for the mishap however as the manager responsible for the breach had already left, they couldn’t undertake any further investigation (Data Protection Commission, n.d.).

To mitigate the issues, I would firstly implement a data retention policy which clearly states how long different types of data collected should be kept. In line with this I would also implement a clear data classification policy and make it mandatory for users to categorise the confidentiality level of the information they are about to store before saving any data. In this case study, if the data was marked as highly confidential it would have increased the chance of it being appropriately stored.

I would secondly introduce a biannual review process of data stored to confirm any data no longer needed is disposed of. Finally, I would introduce a logging software that allows the data controller to track who accesses data and when.

Purpose of Assessment:

A vulnerability assessment is a way of identifying security vulnerabilities. An assessment of security procedures which may indicate potential weaknesses and provide appropriate mitigation procedures (Rahalkar, 2018). By utilizing Center for Internet Security (CIS) and International Organization for Standardization (ISO) controls we would aim to reduce and ultimately eliminate weaknesses to an acceptable risk level based on the enterprises risk appetite.

Method of Assessment:

A combination of CIS and ISO will be used for vulnerability identification and tailored to the enterprise’s organization or purpose. CIS is an information security risk assessment method that aids enterprises in implementing and assessing their security posture against the CIS controls cybersecurity best practices. CIS methodology is applied to assess configuration of workstations, firewalls, and network devices (CIS.org, 2021). ISO methodology involves implementing a risk assessment that can be frequently reviewed and enhanced to assess and correct, identifying the activities for which standards are used. Once completed one can investigate how these activities, supported using standards, contribute to the creation of value for the enterprise, ISO 27,001 is a standard outlining how any business should handle risk at every level (ISO.org, 2013).

Tools to be used in this assessment:

  1. Nmap: Used to map the networks attack surface, this tool scans for hosts, operating systems, hardware versions, software versions and open port vulnerabilities (Nmap.org 2020).
  2. Nessus Vulnerability scanner in conjunction with Metasploit: Nessus as a tool checks all computers, servers, and firewalls for open ports and potentially vulnerable software. Metasploit is used to confirm whether or not the reported holes are exploitable (Oracle.com, 2021).
  3. Burp Suite: Used to scan the security of apps, web browsers and web applications. This tool discovers the application function, scans for security holes and launches custom attacks (Kiprin, 2021).

Business impacts relating to the use of these tools and methods:

  1. Scanning a host with Nmap can take just a fifth of a second, however this time allocation can quickly mount, depending on the number of hosts in an environment. This scan can however be run during office hours with very little to no impact on the network (Nmap.org 2020).
  2. Applications on hosts have been known to crash when a Nessus Vulnerability scanner is taking place, therefore it is recommended to perform a scan of this nature on a production environment after standard working hours, or in an allocated change window. (Tenable.org 2019).
  3. Burp Suite should impose no impact on users as this type of scan has been programmed to find backdoors and run in an undetected manner (Ravello, 2016).

  4. Lack of communication and technical understanding (Hutchins, 2020)

Executive Summary of a Network and Information Security Management (NISM) Assessment

We conducted a comprehensive assessment of the organisation's security environment through vulnerability scanning and penetration testing with a GDPR focused lens. The website scanned for vulnerabilities, is a e-healthcare business’s website which deals with private and sensitive information such as: personally, identifiable information, health information, health records, credit card information and so on. Detailed below is the methodology, findings and recommendations.

Section 1 - Methodology:

A combination of GDPR and ISO was used for vulnerability identification. ISO methodology involves implementing a risk assessment that can be frequently reviewed and enhanced to assess and correct, identifying the activities for which standards are used. ISO 27,001 is a standard outlining how any business should handle risk at every level (ISO.org, 2013).

GDPR is a mandatory requirement for organizations that collect, store and process data that belong to EU Citizens regardless of where the organisation is based. It is important to be aware of the key GDPR principles as it will identify vulnerabilities that could threaten the organisations data, it will enable the organization to adhere to GDPR requirements, avoiding future penalties and as an overall, the findings help the management to recognize and value data protection.

Below are the basic principles of the GDPR which are known as the legal framework of GDPR:

  1. Data minimization: Store or process the minimum amount of personal data to what is only necessary.
  2. Limitation of purpose: Store or process the data type as minimum as possible to serve the purpose only.
  3. Accuracy: Keep personal information up to date and accurate.
  4. Confidentiality and Integrity: Security in storing personal data and in processing the data should be guaranteed. Systems, processes and personnel should be in place to guarantee avoidance of unlawful access, loss or breach of data.
  5. Transparency: Transparency in processing personal data.
  6. Accountability: Organizations storing personal data, should always be accountable to security breaches and must always keep compliant with the GDPR.
  7. Storage limitation: Limit the storage timeline of the data to what is only required, do not store personal data for longer than needed.

Section 4 – Evaluation of website against GDPR and OWASP Standards:

As this website was a health and wellbeing website designed to host the personal information of clients, it is imperative that it meets at least basic security standards. Globally there are many security standards that apply to websites, specific to the US for example is the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) which focuses on personal health information. However the summary will focus on UK regulations. Two security Standards that can be considered here are the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Open Web Application Security Project (OWASP). GDPR encompasses; data capture, tracking, permissions, privacy policies and website security (Taylor, 2018).

A survey completed by McAfee Security, Beyond the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), mcafee.com/beyondGDPR asked businesses what changes they were most concerned about impacting their businesses and reported (as seen below) that GDPR was indeed a top concern.

These results provided significant understanding into individual and organizational approaches in the private healthcare sector toward residency of data, protection of data, and how prepared they are to change and adopt the regulatory requirements.

In evaluating the website aforementioned, numerous tools including (but not limited to) ImmuniWeb and 2GDPR were used to scan for GDPR compliance.

The results indicate that this website meets a majority of the requirements, there has for example, been put in place a sufficient level of cyber security using HTTPS encryption. There was no third-party tracking found and no third-party cookies were detected. However, the website lacks any cookie disclaimer, it is also missing a privacy policy and a listing for consent on the contact form. All three issues fall under the same Article – number 13; “GDPR requires a data controller to provide conspicuously visible notice to data subjects when collecting their personal data, including but not limited to data collected by web applications” (GDPR-info.eu, 2018). To meet GDPR compliance it is important to implement mitigations pertaining to the issues mentioned above. This can be done by ensuring the below after which it is important to rerun compliance tests and verify compliance

  • Develop a privacy policy that is clearly written, concise and easily accessible on the website (HostPapa, 2021).
  • Add a cookie disclaimer banner to the website which visitors would need to agree to, thus granting consent before using any cookies, except strictly necessary ones (Koch, 2021).
  • Add a tick-box to the contact form, which requires visitors to confirm they have read and agree to your terms and privacy policy (Koch, 2021).

The second standard considered is the (OWASP) Top 10. It’s notoriously important to ensure mitigation coverage of common Web Based Vulnerabilities using the OWASP Top 10. This is a standard which represents the most critical security risks related to web applications and focuses on Application Security Verification Standards (ASVS). An OWASP Zed Attack Proxy (ZAP) scan was performed on the website as part of the evaluation’s penetration testing. As a result no critical alerts were generated, however there were some amber security exploits detected. One of the main exposures was the X-frame options header having not been configured, which laid bare a vulnerability to ClickJacking. The timestamp disclosure was also visible and should not have been. A site leaving such an exposure could remain vulnerable to exploitable design access

patterns. The last main vulnerability exposed here was the Cache-Control Header Set not having been configured, this allows cache content to be displayed which is not secure practice.

The above exposures need to be secured to ensure that OWASP standards are effectively incorporated. Mitigating measure can be put in place by

  1. Have management confirm the timestamp disclosure data is not sensitive (Auger, 2010).
  2. Ensure the cache-control HTTP header is set to ‘no-cache’, ‘no-store’, and ‘must-revalidate’ (Mozilla, 2021).

In summary the evaluation shows that this website is not currently GDPR compliant and is potentially exploitable. However, by establishing some select mitigations the goal of compliance is achievable in a relatively short time frame. Ensuring one’s website remains compliant is vital to the cyber security function of any corporation.

Section 5 – Final Conclusions:

The EU has enforced General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) since May 2018. This means all those collecting, storing, using or dealing with personal data of E. U. citizens in any means should be taking all of the GDPR standards into consideration.

Mandating compliance requirements could be troublesome but in the long term, there is always recognition. Client’s and customer’s data privacy and safety always brings happy customers back to the business.

Successfully completing a GDPR audit is the final step toward a GDPR compliance which every company that processes, stores or deals with European citizen’s data should be compliant with. For such organizations, it is also recommended to complete internal audits every 3 to 6 months to ensure continuity with the GDPR compliance.

Reflective Log

I had mixed expectations regarding this Networking and Information Security Management module. I expected that learning about network security would be challenging. Prior to the module, my only knowledge of the network layer was through my foundational cloud infrastructure learning where I understood that networking was one of the core layers (Microsoft Azure, 2019). However, concepts such as the OSI model of physical computing were unknown to me before undertaking this module. Also, I knew that a key part of this course would involve learning about risk assessments and that topic of learning was more comfortable with me due to my work experiences. However, I was most looking forward to learning about the practical methods behind vulnerability and penetration testing as prior to the module I had only had exposure to theoretical approaches. Finally, knowing that an E-Portfolio submission was going to be one of Compulsory outputs for this module made me take a reflective approach whilst completing each unit.

During this module we were tasked with 2 main group outputs. The first being a development team project. We had to create a website to be tested for vulnerability weaknesses by another group. We decided to delegate the task based on strengths so those with the most experience with web development took responsibility for this task. I felt like this was a good approach because it enabled us to get off to a strong start which was supported by Fleming, (2009) who suggests that delegation should be based on an individual’s unique skill set and expertise. The second part of this task involved conducting basic vulnerability scans and deciding which tools would be most appropriate to utilise. The final part was to create a report that summarised the work that we had conducted so far and highlight the potential benefits for the client. This was a beneficial exercise as report writing is a key skill in a practical working environment., We learned how to convey potential challenges and impacts in a manner that was professional and informative. Overall, for the first project I believe the team worked effectively as we managed to meet all deadlines however there was room for improvement when it came to the organisation, we took a siloed approach with each member being delegated at specific task, but it would be beneficial for learning if all members have some involvement in each task. This mirrors a “cross training” approach that some companies take with the aim of developing a workforce that have well rounded skill sets and consequently will be able to understand objectives that aren’t necessarily part of their immediate area (Mosca, Buzza and Abraham, 2007).

The next task was to create an executive summary which would pull together our findings from the vulnerability scanning as well as our well researched approach to mitigating these weaknesses and providing beneficial recommendations to the client. This challenged us to think more critically because we had to tailor our report to our client specific business priority which at this point was compliance with the GDPR regulation. I believe that this is an accurate reflection of challenges that we may have to address in a real working environment as businesses are often subject to a variety of statutory regulations so knowing how to factor these points in when creating solutions is beneficial. Furthermore, we also had the chance to develop our skills of converting technical data into palatable information. McBride, (2018) says that the majority of stakeholders need an effective and understandable translation of our work, so they are well informed, therefore this is a skill that is important to have when dealing with stakeholders who have varied technical knowledge. Regarding the team dynamic, I believe the second project was more cohesive as we had already had experience working with each other and had a better idea of each individual’s skill sets. Furthermore, I pushed myself outside my comfort zone to take a more organisational role and I believe that this benefited the team. Although I was able to delegate work and part of the report to each individual, I also made it a point to ensure that we all fed back to each other and as having oversight of the whole project led to more cohesion and better understanding.

The outcome of these exercises were firstly, a very detailed executive report which all team members had an equal contribution towards. Research shows that it is important for each member to contribute equally as students do not view group assignments as fair if everyone is rewarded equally for unequal levels of contribution (Freeman and McKenzie, 2002). From working as part of the developmental team for this project I learned that it is important for each team member to contribute something to final outputs as this creates a sense of ownership towards the work. Furthermore, although it is beneficial for individuals to take on tasks suited to their skill sets, one thing that I would do is challenge myself to take on tasks that would provide a steep learning curve such as the coding pieces where my technical skills were very limited. pushing yourself outside your comfort zone when it comes to academic tasks has been proven to be rewarding as even if mistakes are made it ultimately is still on opportunity for learning and reflection (Brenner, 2015),

To conclude this module matched my expectations as I got to learn about the history of networking which provided a great background when looking at the tools available today and assessing which would be appropriate. As a team I believe we've developed well from our design proposal in unit 6 to our final report in unit 11 as we built upon the initial scope and made it more specific to our clients and their needs. We had to take into consideration our clients business priorities as well as the approaches that would create the most accurate and relevant output when it comes to network analysis thus highlighting some of the legal and professional challenges that information security professionals must consider when conducting their work.
