Research Methods and Professional Practice - Unit 1

Learning Objective 1

  • Appraise the professional, legal, social, cultural, and ethical issues that affect computing professionals.

Peer Collab Discussion - Initial Post

Corazón, a medical technology start-up developing an implantable heart health monitoring device, showcases ethical considerations in creating and implementing technology (ACM Ethics, 2018).

Corazón's cross jurisdiction compliance with medical device regulations demonstrates their commitment to the BCS Code of Conduct which requires professionals to prioritise the interests of society whilst working within the law, (BCS, 2018). However, a vulnerability in wireless connectivity could result in legal action against the company if patent data is compromised.

Providing free or reduced access to healthcare for patients below the poverty line is a commendable effort from a social perspective. Nonetheless, any patient data breach may weaken the public's trust in the company and its commitment to safeguarding patient information.

Corazón's open bug bounty program and willingness to conduct independent security evaluations demonstrate their commitment to best practices and addressing potential vulnerabilities in a responsible and ethical manner. While their prompt response to the recent security issue exemplifies their adherence to the BCS standard to “respect and value alternative viewpoints and seek, accept and offer honest criticisms to work” their use of a hard-coded value in the implant raises concerns about the thoroughness of their risk analysis and highlights the need for ongoing evaluation and improvement in their technology products.
